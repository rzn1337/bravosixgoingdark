Today felt like a slow build rather than a sprint. I opened the folder of
older notes and read through a few of them to remember where I had left off.
Some of the questions I wrote weeks ago now have obvious answers, which is a
quiet kind of encouragement. I moved the finished items into the archive and
left the open ones near the top.

The middle of the day was the most productive. I drafted a rough version
without worrying whether it was any good, then went back and tightened the
weak spots. Writing the messy first pass always feels harder than it should,
but once the words are on the page the editing goes quickly.

I took a short break to clear my head and came back with a clearer sense of the
next step. It is strange how often the answer appears the moment I stop
searching for it. I kept the notes brief and dated each one so I can trace the
thread again tomorrow without much effort.

By evening the desk was tidy and the list was shorter than when I started.
That is enough to call it a good day. I am learning to measure progress by the
direction of travel rather than the distance covered, and that small shift has
made the work feel lighter.
